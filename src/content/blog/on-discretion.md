I'm often asked why I keep certain details close — a city, a name, a night. The honest answer is that discretion isn't secrecy for its own sake; it's how I protect the people and places that matter to me.

There's a particular kind of trust built in what's *not* said. The people who know me understand this instinctively, and I've found that the ones worth knowing usually do.

So if a caption feels incomplete, it probably is — on purpose.
